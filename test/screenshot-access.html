A short passage

By the Garden Wall

Late afternoon, somewhere quiet

Beyond the orchard wall, where the path grows narrow and the grass remembers every footstep, there is a hidden access to the river. No sign marks it; only the cedars lean aside, as if they have kept the secret for years.

You hear the water before you see it — a low, steady turning under willow shade. Light falls in pieces through the leaves. For a moment the city feels far away, and the afternoon holds still, as though it too had found the way in.
